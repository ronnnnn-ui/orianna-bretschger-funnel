The Problem

Industrial Wastewater Is Bleeding Your Budget — and Threatening Your License to Operate

Most facilities are locked into aging infrastructure, unpredictable sewer surcharges, and compliance risks they can't fully control. Here's what it's actually costing you:

Runaway Sewer Surcharges

High-strength wastewater from food & beverage, breweries, and distilleries carries BOD loads that trigger punishing municipal surcharges — costs that compound as you grow production.

Permit Violations & Compliance Risk

Discharge standards are tightening. One violation can trigger fines, operational shutdowns, or forced infrastructure overhauls — all while your core business grinds to a halt.

Infrastructure Too Rigid to Scale

Traditional treatment systems are massive capital investments that can't flex with production. When you expand, you're locked into expensive upgrades — or expensive hauling to fill the gap.

The BETT® Technology

BioElectrochemical Treatment That Treats the Waste, Cuts the Cost, and Cleans the Air.

BETT® uses locally-sourced microbes in modular, shipping-container-sized reactors that consume organic waste, generate electricity, and deliver industrial-grade treatment — on-site, at scale, guaranteed.

Modular & Plug-and-Play

Each BETT® unit fits in a standard 40-ft shipping container, removing 800–3,000 lbs of BOD per day. Stack units to match your treatment needs — scale up without rebuilding infrastructure.

24/7 Remote Monitoring

Aquacycl's team monitors your system remotely in real-time — tracking microbe health, treatment efficiency, and compliance data so your team doesn't have to.

100% Permit Compliance Guarantee

Aquacycl guarantees compliance with your discharge permits. If the system doesn't meet standards, we fix it — no additional cost to you. No excuses. Just clean water.

Zero Capital Outlay — WaaS Model

Wastewater as a Service: Aquacycl installs, operates, and maintains the entire system. You pay for treatment performance, not equipment. Operational from day 60.

The Founder

Orianna Bretschger

CEO & Co-Founder — Aquacycl

Orianna grew up in remote areas of the southwestern United States without running water or electricity — relying on outhouses for sanitation. Those formative experiences ignited a lifelong commitment to solving water access challenges that most people never see.

She studied materials science and engineering at USC, where she discovered that electrochemical bacteria could generate electricity while cleaning water. After 12 years of research at the J. Craig Venter Institute developing the science behind how bacteria breathe and eat, she and co-founder Sofia Babanova launched Aquacycl in 2016 to commercialize it.

Today, Aquacycl is Series B-funded, named an Earthshot Prize Finalist, and recognized by the Cartier Women's Initiative — with BETT® systems deployed across food & beverage manufacturers globally.
